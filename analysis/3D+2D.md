# 3D+2D Extension

There is 1 possibility: 

## $\mathfrak{s}_{3,2}^{0,1}$

$\mathfrak{s}_{3,1}^{0,1}$ is defined by
$$
\begin{bmatrix}
2 & 0 & 0 \\
0 & 1 & 0 \\
0 & 0 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
,
\begin{bmatrix}
0 & 0 & 0 \\
0 & 1 & 0 \\
0 & 0 & -1
\end{bmatrix}
$$
Then 
$$
Q_{\mathfrak{s}_{3,2}^{0,1}}(z) = z_0^2(z_0 + 2z_5)(z_0 + z_4 - z_5)(z_0 + z_4 + z_5)
$$
so 
$$
k(\mathfrak{s}_{3,2}^{0,1})=4.
$$
